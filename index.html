Dr. Jose Rizal

A Hero's Legacy

Bibliography:

José Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonzo Realonda was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna. He was the seventh of eleven children born to Francisco Mercado and Teodora Alonso. Rizal showed early signs of brilliance and later pursued studies in Manila and Europe, excelling in medicine, literature, and philosophy.

Major Works & Contributions:

  • Noli Me Tangere (1887): Exposed the social injustices under Spanish rule.
  • El Filibusterismo (1891): A darker sequel that expressed frustration with the status quo.
  • Advocated for education, equal rights, and peaceful reform.
  • Opposed oppression, but rejected violent revolution.

Legacy:

On December 30, 1896, José Rizal was executed in Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park). His death sparked greater resistance and helped fuel the Philippine revolution. Today, Rizal is remembered through monuments, schools, currency, and a national holiday.

"The youth is the hope of our future." – Dr. José Rizal

Rizal’s life reminds us of the power of words, courage, and intellect in creating change. His enduring influence continues to shape Filipino identity and pride.
